Each row shows how a mid-sleep awakening (WakeEvent) modifies the existing two-layer cell rule. Mid-sleep awakening cells use the same visual treatment as "bookend" in-bed-awake cells — red bottom, no hatch — because they are the same physical state (in bed, not asleep). The wake-event distinction lives in the data model and the notes summary, not in the cell color.

Cell rule (extended):
  • Bottom red bar drawn iff [bedInAt, bedOutAt] overlaps the cell (unchanged)
  • Top diagonal hatch drawn iff [asleepAt, awakeAt] overlaps the cell AND no WakeEvent overlaps it
Cell visualization: bookend awake-in-bed cells (e.g., lying awake at 5/4 hour 7 before getting up) and mid-sleep awakening cells (5/4 hour 3) look identical — both are red-only with no hatch — because they're the same physical state: in bed, not asleep. The mid-sleep awakening signal lives in the notes column, not in the cell color.

Notes column: auto-prepends 覚醒×N (M分) / Wakes×N (Mm) when there is at least one wake event, followed by the user's typed note on the next line. A doctor scanning a month of charts spots interrupted nights from the notes summary, and the chart row visually shows the interruptions as red gaps in the hatch.
